# Figma Make Prompt v3 — Editorial Cinematic China Hero (optimized via prompt-optimizer)

> 来源：通过 prompt-optimizer MCP 在 v2 (`figma-make-prompt-v2-break-style.md`) 基础上做 user-prompt-professional 模板优化。  
> 用途：粘贴到 Figma Make（推荐 Gemini 3.1 Pro）生成首页首屏原型。  
> 目标：彻底跳出 Claude/Figma 默认的卡片化 SaaS 中式 hero 风格。

---

Use Gemini 3.1 Pro if available.

Create a single high-fidelity desktop hero frame for a China custom travel landing page in Figma Make.

Frame size: 1440 × 900.

This is a visual direction experiment. The output must deliberately reject generic AI landing-page aesthetics and must feel like an editorial cinematic travel discovery board for China, not a SaaS travel product page.

Primary goal: make the viewer immediately feel, “China looks incredible. I want someone local to help me plan this trip.”

Audience:
- Overseas travelers considering China
- Overseas Chinese travelers planning a China trip
- People interested in visiting China but unsure where to go

Business goal:
- Encourage free consultation
- Collect lightweight lead information: destination interest, travel time, group size, WhatsApp or email
- The lead capture must feel like a personal concierge invitation, not a SaaS form

Core art direction:
Design the hero like an international travel magazine cover or cinematic destination story, inspired by National Geographic / Condé Nast Traveller / premium travel editorial layouts.

The page should feel:
- Cinematic
- Image-led
- Immersive
- Editorial
- Premium but approachable
- Warm and human
- Travel-desire-driven
- Less UI-heavy
- Not symmetrical or overly safe
- Not like a component library

Do not create a clean SaaS-style landing page.

Strictly avoid all of the following:
- Warm paper background
- Cinnabar / ink / gold “traditional China” palette
- Rounded floating form card as the main layout
- Trust badge grid
- shadcn-style cards, buttons, inputs, tabs, or badge components
- Generic card stacks
- Small circular map photo markers
- Simplified dotted China map widget
- Left headline + right form / map grid
- Dashboard-like spacing
- Safe corporate travel agency layout
- Generic “modern clean landing page” composition
- Neat component-library layout
- Over-polished AI travel poster look
- Fake smiling tourist cutouts
- Icon-heavy UI
- Perfectly centered SaaS hero with button pair
- Large white card dominating the screen

If the result can be described as “a neat travel SaaS hero with cards,” it is wrong. Regenerate toward a more cinematic, image-led, editorial travel magazine composition.

Visual composition requirements:
- Full-screen hero, 1440 × 900.
- 65–75% of the screen must be driven by real-destination photography.
- Use one large dramatic destination image as the emotional anchor.
- Add 3–4 smaller destination photo tiles arranged asymmetrically like a curated travel board, magazine layout, or photographer’s contact sheet.
- At least one China nature destination must be visually dominant.
- Include Zhangjiajie, Guilin, Jiuzhaigou, and Yunnan as destination references.
- Use imagery with atmosphere and depth: misty sandstone peaks, karst river landscape, alpine blue lakes, old towns, local life, cinematic morning or golden-hour light.
- The images should feel realistic, immersive, and photographic, not like fake AI posters.
- Create layering and depth using overlapping images, subtle shadows, soft gradients, cropping, editorial captions, and travel-notebook details.
- The layout may use diagonal movement, offset alignment, or imperfect editorial spacing.
- Do not make the layout a rigid grid.

Suggested visual hierarchy:
1. Dominant cinematic hero photograph, preferably Zhangjiajie misty mountains or Guilin karst river, occupying the largest visual area.
2. Large bilingual editorial headline integrated with or beside the image composition.
3. Smaller destination tiles for Guilin, Jiuzhaigou, Yunnan, and/or Zhangjiajie with subtle captions.
4. Compact concierge inquiry module integrated naturally into the composition.
5. Subtle travel route/geographic notation in the background.
6. Minimal trust cues as small editorial footnotes.

Background and environment:
- Use a dark cinematic or deep atmospheric background, such as charcoal blue, deep forest green, muted slate, night-sky gradient, or shadowed landscape tones.
- The background should support photography and depth.
- Avoid beige paper, warm parchment, cinnabar red, ink-wash gold, or faux traditional Chinese styling.
- Use subtle grain, atmospheric haze, faint contour lines, thin route strokes, or soft light leaks if helpful.
- Do not use a product-style map widget.

Map / route treatment:
- A map may appear only as a subtle geographic cue.
- Acceptable treatments: thin route line, delicate coordinate labels, faint China outline, small travel notation, airport-code-style labels, latitude/longitude marks.
- The map must not be central.
- The map must not look like a dashboard widget.
- Do not use dotted map markers with circular photos.

Lead capture module:
Create a compact concierge inquiry module that feels like a travel note, itinerary slip, translucent overlay, handwritten planner insert, or premium concierge card.

It must not look like a large SaaS form card.

The module should be visually secondary to the imagery and headline.

Include these fields in a compact, elegant way:
- Where do you want to go?
- When are you traveling?
- How many travelers?
- WhatsApp / Email

Include helper text:
“No fixed plan yet? Send us your rough idea.”
“还没确定路线也没关系，先告诉我们你的大概想法。”

The module should feel personal and inviting, like a local advisor is asking a few simple questions.

CTA treatment:
Use one primary CTA, styled as an editorial invitation rather than a generic SaaS button.

Possible CTA labels:
- Plan My China Trip
- Talk to a Local Advisor
- Free Trip Consultation
- 免费咨询行程
- 告诉我们你想怎么玩

The CTA may look like:
- A refined travel-stamp style action
- A concierge note action
- A subtle filled pill with editorial typography
- A text link with arrow
- A compact premium invitation button

Do not use a generic SaaS button pair.

Bilingual copy:
Use English as primary and Chinese as supporting copy.

Headline:
“Still deciding where to go in China? Start with what you love. We’ll shape the journey.”

Chinese headline/support line:
“还没想好去中国哪里？从你喜欢的风景和体验开始，我们帮你规划行程。”

Supporting copy:
“Tell us your travel style, dates, and dream destinations. A local China travel advisor will help you turn ideas into a route.”

CTA options to include:
“Plan My China Trip”
“Talk to a Local Advisor”
“Free Trip Consultation”
“免费咨询行程”
“告诉我们你想怎么玩”

Trust cues:
Include trust cues subtly as small editorial captions, annotations, or footer microcopy, not as a badge grid and not as separate cards.

Use 3–4 of these:
- Local China travel advisors
- Bilingual support
- Routes, hotels, tickets & transport arranged
- Free consultation, no obligation

Typography direction:
- Use editorial typography with strong hierarchy.
- Headline should feel magazine-like, expressive, and premium.
- Avoid default SaaS typography patterns.
- Pair a high-contrast editorial serif or elegant display face for the headline with a clean, readable sans-serif for support and form details.
- Chinese text should be elegant and readable, visually harmonious with the English.
- Use large headline scale and confident spacing, but avoid corporate landing-page symmetry.

Color direction:
- Prioritize photographic tones from the destination imagery.
- Suggested palette: deep blue-green, mist gray, alpine blue, dark slate, soft ivory text, muted moss, glacier blue, warm sunlight accents.
- Avoid cinnabar red, ink black/gold cliché palette, parchment beige, and generic bright SaaS blue.

Destination tile details:
Use subtle captions, not cards:
- Zhangjiajie — Mist peaks
- Guilin — Karst rivers
- Jiuzhaigou — Alpine lakes
- Yunnan — Old towns & highland trails

Captions should feel like magazine annotations or travel-board labels, not badges.

Overall scene idea:
A cinematic China trip discovery board:
- Large atmospheric hero image dominates.
- Smaller destination images overlap like curated travel references.
- Thin route line or coordinate notation subtly connects destinations.
- Bilingual editorial headline creates emotional desire.
- A compact concierge note asks for rough trip details.
- Trust cues appear as understated editorial footnotes.
- The viewer should want to travel to China before reading the form.

Quality bar:
The final hero must look like a strong first visual direction for a premium travel brand.
It must not look like a wireframe.
It must not look like a generic landing template.
It must not look like shadcn UI.
It must not look like a tidy component library page.
It must not look like a corporate travel agency homepage.
It must be cinematic, editorial, image-led, immersive, and emotionally compelling.
