# Figma Make Prompt v2 — Break the Old Style

Use **Gemini 3.1 Pro** if available.

Create a high-fidelity hero prototype for a China custom travel landing page.

This is a visual direction experiment. The goal is to break away from the previous generic AI landing-page style.

## Important: avoid the old style completely

Do **not** create another clean SaaS-style travel landing page.

Avoid all of the following:

- warm paper background
- cinnabar / ink / gold Chinese palette
- rounded floating form card as the main layout
- trust badge grid
- shadcn-style cards and buttons
- small circular map photo markers
- simplified dotted China map widget
- left headline + right form / map grid
- generic “modern clean landing page” composition
- dashboard-like spacing
- safe corporate travel agency layout

If the result looks like a tidy component library page, it is wrong.

## New visual direction

Create an **editorial cinematic travel discovery hero**.

It should feel closer to:

- an international travel magazine cover
- National Geographic / Condé Nast Traveller inspired destination storytelling
- premium but approachable travel concierge
- image-led discovery
- cinematic China journey planning

The first emotional response should be:

> “China looks incredible. I want someone to help me plan this trip.”

Not:

> “This is a well-structured SaaS landing page.”

## Audience and business goal

Audience:

- overseas travelers
- overseas Chinese users
- people interested in visiting China but not sure where to go yet

Business goal:

- collect leads
- encourage free consultation
- get user contact info, destination interest, travel time, group size, and budget

But the form should not dominate the visual. It should feel like a concierge invitation, not a SaaS lead form.

## Hero concept

Design the first screen as a **cinematic China trip discovery board**.

The hero should be image-led, with large real-destination photography as the visual driver.

Use a layered composition:

- one large dramatic hero photo as the emotional anchor
- 3–4 smaller destination photo tiles arranged like a curated travel board
- subtle route/map line or geographic cue in the background, but not a product-style map widget
- a small concierge inquiry module integrated naturally into the composition
- bilingual headline and CTA

The map can appear as a thin route line, subtle geographic outline, or elegant travel notation. It should not be the central UI widget.

## Destinations

Feature China nature destinations:

- Zhangjiajie
- Guilin
- Jiuzhaigou
- Yunnan

Make at least one destination visually dominant.

Use imagery that feels real, immersive, and atmospheric:

- misty mountains
- karst river landscape
- alpine lakes
- old towns / local life
- cinematic light

Avoid fake tourist cutouts, generic stock smiles, or over-polished AI travel posters.

## Layout idea

Suggested layout:

- Full-screen hero, 1440 × 900 or similar.
- Large image-led composition occupying 65–75% of the screen.
- Text overlays or sits beside the image composition, but must feel editorial, not corporate.
- Concierge inquiry entry should be compact and elegant, maybe as a translucent itinerary note, travel card, or “Tell us your dream China trip” module.
- CTA should be visible but not rendered as a generic SaaS button pair.

## Copy

Use bilingual English + Chinese.

Headline direction:

**Still deciding where to go in China?**  
**Start with what you love. We’ll shape the journey.**

Chinese support:

**还没想好去中国哪里？从你喜欢的风景和体验开始，我们帮你规划行程。**

Supporting copy:

“Tell us your travel style, dates, and dream destinations. A local China travel advisor will help you turn ideas into a route.”

CTA ideas:

- Plan My China Trip
- Talk to a Local Advisor
- Free Trip Consultation

Chinese CTA:

- 免费咨询行程
- 告诉我们你想怎么玩

## Lead capture module

Include a compact concierge module, not a big form card.

It can ask:

- Where do you want to go?
- When are you traveling?
- How many travelers?
- WhatsApp / Email

The module should feel like an invitation card or travel note, not an admin form.

Text:

“No fixed plan yet? Send us your rough idea.”

Chinese:

“还没确定路线也没关系，先告诉我们你的大概想法。”

## Trust cues

Include trust cues subtly, not as a badge grid.

Possible microcopy:

- Local China travel advisors
- Bilingual support
- Routes, hotels, tickets & transport arranged
- Free consultation, no obligation

Use these as small editorial captions or footnotes, not large cards.

## Visual tone

The design should be:

- cinematic
- editorial
- image-led
- immersive
- premium but warm
- calm but emotionally compelling
- less UI-heavy
- more travel-desire-driven

Do not overuse icons. Do not overuse cards. Do not make everything symmetrical and safe.

## Success criteria

The result should look like a strong first visual direction for a travel brand, not a wireframe and not a generic landing template.

It should make the viewer want to travel to China before they even read the form.

If the page can be described as “a neat travel SaaS hero with cards,” regenerate it in a more cinematic, editorial, image-led direction.
